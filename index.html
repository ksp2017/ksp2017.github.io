Grainger Library Bans Non-Engineering Students

In an effort to appease engineering students who are demanding more study space, Grainger Library, per the request of the College of Engineering, has decided to close its doors to non-engineering students. Representatives from the College state that this is simply the most economic solution because "engineering students need more space to study and complete school work as the college continues to grow and simply limiting access to our existing facilities will not require the investment of additional space."

"Engineering students pay an elevated tuition which should guarantee them the right to use College of Engineering facilities freely without worrying that students in other majors are filling up limited space." --Engineering Official

When asked if they felt that this was unfair since over 70% of the student body does not study engineering, officials stated that "engineering students pay an elevated tuition which should guarantee them the right to use College of Engineering facilities freely without worrying that students in other majors are filling up limited space" and that "non-engineering students should simply go to the UGL if they need to study." The University has questioned this decision, which they feel could harm the inclusive feel UIUC aims to achieve. In response, Grainger has agreed to install the UGL doors on all entrances, which they hope will lessen the appeal of Grainger Library as a study space for non-engineers.
Dean Cangellaris to visit Playboy Mansion

Dean Andreas Cangellaris launched the Engineering Visionary Scholarship Initiative last May with a goal to raise $100 million endowment to bring the nation’s best students to Engineering at Illinois. The Office of Advancement has several upcoming regional launch events staged to take place within the upcoming months. To reach the $100 million goal, Cangellaris has a lot of hard work to do, but he hopes to achieve his goal with the help of alumni. Dean Cangellaris has scheduled the first trip to visit famous alum Hugh Hefner who has a net worth of about $43 million. Originally from Chicago, Hefner graduated from the University of Illinois with a B.A. in psychology with a double minor in creative writing and art in 1949.

The trip organized by the Office of Advancement and personally headed by Dean Cangellaris will feature a three day stay at the famous playboy mansion in Los Angeles, California from March 26-28th. Dean Cangellaris scheduled the trip during Spring Break to encourage student participation. Dean Cangellaris’s goal is to encourage Hefner now 88 years old to consider the University of Illinois in his will. Dean Cangellaris is also open to the idea of naming the new ECE building after Hefner at the "right price". The Office of Advancement plans to fully fund 15-20 students interested in attending the outreach event. The itinerary includes meeting with the staff of Playboy magazine, a tour of the mansion, and a "networking session" at the end of the 3 day trip. Hefner plans to feature the University of Illinois visit in the May issue of Playboy magazine.

Students interested in the outreach trip can apply at http://tinyurl.com/PlayboyUIUC by Friday March 6th.
Classes Cancelled on Unofficial: Administration Makes Truce

A recent decision made by the University of Illinois will impact most of the student body. School officials have decided to cave into student requests and cancel all classes on Unofficial. "We give up, it’s just too hard to try to get students to come to class on Unofficial," one U of I official states. While the school has always been very strict on Unofficial policies, this year they have just given up. All classes campus-wide will be cancelled on March 6th, so students can enjoy Unofficial without worrying about lectures and discussion sections.

University Housing is also lifting their strict no guest policy because they "want students to have a good time and just enjoy themselves," says an RA from the six pack. So students, wake up at the crack of dawn, wear green, and join in the festivities because you won’t have to go to class!
ECE Department moves back to Everitt Lab

This past year has been one of ups and downs for the ECE Department. 2014 heralded the start of a modern era for ECE - with the move to a massive new building, it was expected that the department would start a fresh chapter with new initiatives and higher levels of motivation. However, despite the millions of dollars and decades of bit flipping that went into this marvelous achievement, things have not quite gone - as some would say - "according to plan".

After a few days of initial excitement and free food, murmurs of discontent and passive-aggressive tweets began to emerge from the ECE community. It seemed that the new building was not quite what everyone expected it to be, and some would say, not much of an improvement over the previous ECE home of Everitt Laboratory. By the time October rolled around, students who had been eager to spend their days eating, breathing, and sleeping in its various spaces were forced to conclude that there was not a single sleepable location in the building. The post-modern furnishings seem to be designed to prevent even the slightest of naps between classes, and the student center itself was left barren of anything that resembled a couch.

Another serious concern among members of the student body was the new building’s elevated TTC rating (Time To Cravings). Everitt Hall boasts an average TTC of 23.7 seconds door-to-door, the best rating of any University building aside from that strange Optical Physics building next to Jerusalem. Unfortunately for members of the ECE Department, the TTC of the new building clocks in at a pitiful 6 minutes and 33.1 seconds, assuming optimal stoplight conditions. According to ECE Senior Abhnav Chelupa, this is simply unacceptable:

"I miss the Malaysian man at Cravings! Do you know how long it has been since I have had hot and spicy chicken from there? … [I] miss the smell of asian food in the computer labs. This just doesn’t feel like home." --Abhnav Chelupa

As a result, many students have resorted to ordering alternative food options delivered to ECEB, such as Jimmy Johns and Taco Bell. However, this does not come without its own difficulties, as numerous students and faculty have complained about having poor cell reception in the building. This can make it very challenging, if not impossible, to contact restaurants in search of deliverable food. Moreover, the disappointing cell coverage has presented a host of other unforeseen consequences for certain members of the department:

"I can never tell if I have new matches on Tinder whenever I am in the ECE Building" says ECE Poster Boy and Master’s Student Ankit Jain, who relies heavily on Tinder’s real-time text updates to maximize his performance on the platform. "It is so hard to keep up with my online image whenever I am in the building. Do you know how many important notifications I receive via text messages? A LOT!!!! I wanna go back!!"

Perhaps the worst experience in the building is felt by the leader and Department Head of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Professor William (Bill) Sanders. Sanders, whose previous office was located on the first floor of Everitt, now has his office on the second floor of the ECE Building, with a huge window facing the quad. He does not like the fact that he has to take the elevator up to his office, which operates at a speed of one floor per hour for safety purposes.

Times were so much simpler when I would enter the building and head straight into my office without the worry of navigating up a whole floor. Now, I have to take an elevator which if I take at noon, doesn’t get me up there till 1pm. Do you know I miss my lunch hour because of that?" --William Sanders

According to Sanders, the department was actually considering putting study tables in the elevators so that students who work on the upper floors of ECEB can use their commute time productively.

But alas, with so many issues cropping up and complaints coming from every pristine corner of the building, the department decided last Friday to move back into Everitt Lab. This mass-migration to the old building marks a return to the old ways for the department. The screams of Ankit Jain yelling "YES!! TINDER BABY HERE I COME!" could be heard echoing in the hallways of ECEB after the department announced the decision to the public. Indeed, Cravings has reported that business is booming - they have completed more orders this past weekend than in all of the fall semester combined.

With the $95 million building now vacant, Dean Cangellaris faces the challenge of deciding how best to allocate the resources of the old new ECE building. He is contemplating giving the space to the Department of Bioengineering, who for some reason have an affinity for dwelling in buildings previously inhabited by computer-type engineers. However, there are also rumors circulating that ECEB could become the long-fabled Business outpost north of Green Street - a BIF 2.0 of sorts. Should this indeed come to pass, the members of the ECE department who so vocally criticized the new building may regret their harsh opinions...
CS Department forms Maximillian Q. Levchin College of Computer Science

Last Thursday, the Department of Computer Science officially announced it would be annexed from the College of Engineering to form the Maximillian Q. Levchin College of Computer Science. The department’s massive growth in the past four years has stressed an already tenuous relationship as space in Siebel Center has grown tighter. Thanks to a generous donation from famous alumnus Max Levchin, the Chancellor's office agreed to make the split. "As our own college, we will have the right to remove pesky ECE students and University High School students from our walls," said newly promoted Dean of Computer Science Lenny Rutenbar.

"Our number one student complaint was how difficult it was to access the classes they wanted to take. Now, we are free to evict other majors from our building and put our students in the classes they need". --Lenny Rutenbar

Rutenbar’s plans are ambitious. Not only will the Maximillian Q. Levchin College of Computer Science offer a degree program in Computer Science, but it will also offer a wide variety of other majors from across campus. Cynthia Gleason, the college’s external relations coordinator, explains: "All of the research on campus can be done better and faster by computer scientists. After a detailed analysis, we believe we can even optimize art history by upwards of 70%". And Rutenbar’s first act as dean? "We have to fix the coffee. The old college’s Bevande Cafe has been issued their two weeks notice, and our very own Starbucks™ is imminent".

The consensus among students is generally positive. "We always knew we were better than other majors," said Mulan Khandekar, a praised CS teaching assistant. "The queue during office hours used to be 40 people long. Now that we are our own college, I should be able to get help within a few minutes." Others were in it for the coffee. "I can’t wait for the Starbucks™. Normally I brew my own roast, but it will be nice to have a real cappuccino without leaving the building," said Cole Coleman, a CS junior.

"I can’t wait for the Starbucks™. Normally I brew my own roast, but it will be nice to have a real cappuccino without leaving the building." --Cole Coleman

For students outside the college, the reaction to the ban on ECE students has been less pleasant. ECE sophomore Braedon Aranguiz elaborated. "We’re moving back to Everitt, and it’s depressing. There are no windows. I was planning to head to Siebel to do my homework in natural light, but now I don’t know what to do."

The switch will officially be announced next Monday, and the ECE ban begins Tuesday at midnight. Course changes will occur immediately afterwards.
Bioengineering Department to Conduct Research on Campus Squirrels

The Department of Bioengineering is launching a new initiative to map the genome of the campus squirrels. Students are very familiar with the squirrel’s unusually outgoing personalities. Said one student, "I legit saw a squirrel shakedown this kid for a Cheeto."

"I legit saw a squirrel shakedown this kid for a Cheeto." --UIUC Student

Now, state of the art techniques will allow the Bioengineering department to determine exactly what is making those squirrels so bold compared to their furry counterparts across the world. The project, which is funded by the $100 million Grainger Grant, will map the squirrel genome and determine what genes are leading to their gregarious behavior. Following this, a second study on human-squirrel interactions will be performed. The Bioengineering department is currently seeking volunteers for this study. Interested students can visit http://archives.library.illinois.edu/blog/squirrels/.

College of Business Loses Corporate Funding

Students within the College of Business are outraged as Top 4 accounting firms decide not to hire business students any longer. They have all realized that engineering students can do the job not only better but faster. These companies use technology that engineers can understand more quickly and easily than business students. These Top 4 accounting firms have pulled out of the Business Career Fair for Fall 2015 and all future Business Career Fairs. They have been working to secure spots in both EXPO and the ECS Career Fair for next fall.

The College of Business has lost nearly all of its corporate funding to the College of Engineering. Due to this large loss of funding, the College of Business will be shutting down after this school year. Students who were planning on transferring into the College of Business should now consider transferring schools or just switching into engineering.

Umberto Ravaioli added to Basketball Team Roster

Coach Groce is not having an easy year. After leading scorer and rebounder Rayvonte Rice took an early leave to the season with a broken hand and Aaron Crosby tore his retina, the Illini Men’s Basketball Team is looking a little sparse. The team had a spark by dressing Student Manager Ryan Schmidt, but after a hard foul in the Michigan game put him out for the rest of the season, hopes have since fizzled.

Luckily for Groce, the UIUC College of Engineering Undergraduate Programs Office has a hidden star: Assistant Dean Umberto Ravaioli. Before relocating to the cornfields of Central Illinois, Ravaioli was an all-star race-walker in Italy. When asked for a comment, Ravaioli said "it’s been a while since [I’ve] race-walked, but 15 years of the sport has put me in great shape. There are many similarities between basketball and race-walking, like the use of your legs". Many faculty and staff in the College of Engineering are supportive of the Assistant Dean’s decision to play. "I honestly can’t think of anyone more qualified," commented Ndu Egekeze, Corporate Liaison for IEFX and former Forward on the Penn State Nittany Lions Division I Men’s Basketball Team. "Since he got the call from Coach Groce, he’s been walking laps around the office. I think he’s really going to be a game changer."
