Grainger Library Bans Non-Engineering Students

In an effort to appease engineering students who are demanding more study space, Grainger Library, per the request of the College of Engineering, has decided to close its doors to non-engineering students. Representatives from the College state that this is simply the most economic solution because "engineering students need more space to study and complete school work as the college continues to grow and simply limiting access to our existing facilities will not require the investment of additional space."

"Engineering students pay an elevated tuition which should guarantee them the right to use College of Engineering facilities freely without worrying that students in other majors are filling up limited space." --Engineering Official

When asked if they felt that this was unfair since over 70% of the student body does not study engineering, officials stated that "engineering students pay an elevated tuition which should guarantee them the right to use College of Engineering facilities freely without worrying that students in other majors are filling up limited space" and that "non-engineering students should simply go to the UGL if they need to study." The University has questioned this decision, which they feel could harm the inclusive feel UIUC aims to achieve. In response, Grainger has agreed to install the UGL doors on all entrances, which they hope will lessen the appeal of Grainger Library as a study space for non-engineers.
